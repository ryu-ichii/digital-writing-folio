# Process Documentation and Project Iteration
## Week 6
### Progress Audit
My early concept revolves around the reader's experience of going through the processes of ordinary life before falling into an inevitable and irreversible state of psychosis. The reader begins in the doctor's office before moving into an interactive but randomised exploration of mental decay before being brought back to the doctor’s office in a cyclical manner. 

The current state of my interactivity map is pictured below. What I have done so far adopts an external-exploratory mode of interactivity in which the reader identifies with the protagonist in the first-person perspective. I do plan on subverting this mode of interactivity throughout the work, especially as the psychosis begins to become more pronounced and take a stranglehold on the protagonist. I want the mode of interactivity to spiral, and the control the reader holds over the plot to disintegrate as it progresses.

In terms of narration, I hope to move between several approaches and as Aarseth (1997) outlines in reference to *Afternoon*, sabotage and derail the narrative. As the psychosis pronounces itself, it should seek to work against complete coherence and understanding. I want the psychosis the subvert and disrupt the comfortability and flow of more traditional narration. It’s in these moments that I might consider addressing the reader directly or write with disconnect between myself the narrator. I want to progressively introduce a breakdown in communication and explore the futility of language itself. The breakdown and limitations of communication seek to further underline the absurdism of both psychosis and life altogether. 

Ergodic Literature as described by Aarseth (1997), is a mode of literature in which the reader is required to partake in ‘nontrivial effort’ (beyond the turning of a page) to consume and traverse the text. I feel the current vision for my work is to adopt the three levels that Aarseth (1997) speaks of. I describe the events and places the reader goes through while narrating alongside the ‘ergodics’ of the reader. Although the decisions of the reader do not alter the world, they do make the reader produce a narrative from what will become a seemingly random sequence of events. The randomness, alongside the choices and decisions offered to the reader, induce nontrivial effort that is not involved in the conventional act of reading. The reader’s decisions will falter against the randomisation and futility of the narrative structure. 

Visually, I have a clear vision of the work’s aesthetics. The most important role of the design is to assist the text and other elements in effectively emulating the thoughts and sensations of psychosis. I want the animations, the images, and videos to come together and displace the reader. I have explored (and will continue to explore) using GIFs, flashes of colour, and random text movement to achieve this. I have been using images from Wikimedia Commons and editing them to appear more surreal and disjointed with a free picture editor online. The images are carefully picked to further pronounce the nightmarish quality to work. I will continue to explore the visual aspect and what can be done in TWINE to ensure I explore all possible avenues of visual communication.

## Week 7
### Progress Audit
I have essentially completed all the tasks I set out to achieve. I have a new and more accurate interactivity architecture map that I’ve attached below. As I’ve built the TWINE document, a lot of the interactivity architecture has altered as I’m sure will continue to happen. Through the creation process, I’ve found certain pathways are unrealistic and clumsy to achieve. I’ve addressed some issues and inconsistencies in how a reader traverses the work. I’ve also added more branches from the key plot points. I like the idea of returning to these key points and being offered a false way out before losing control again. I am much happier with this new map and believe it offers a more complete vision of the events and rooms of the work. 

Through remapping and refining my understanding of the work, I have a much better idea of how it functions and how it escalates over time. I’m also far more aware of the limitations of my project thanks to becoming more familiar with TWINE Harlowe. I’ve decided to use TWINE as I’ve familiarised myself with it through experimentation both in class and at home. I have over four rooms complete but continue to tweak their design and formatting elements. I am yet to be fully satisfied with them but am enjoying configuring soundtracks, sound FX, and background images. I’ve begun to explore animations and moving textboxes, that can assist in conveying an uncertain and emotional draining state of being. I am still figuring out how to realise my goals of randomisation by trawling through the guidebooks and forums. I have established a few methods for achieving this, that I will have to experiment with further as I continue to bring the work to life.
