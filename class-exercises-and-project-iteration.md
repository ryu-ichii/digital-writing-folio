## Week 7
### Progress Audit
I have completed all the tasks I set out to achieve last week. I have a new and more accurate interactivity architecture map that I’ve attached below. As I’ve built the TWINE document, a lot of the interactivity architecture has altered significantly, as I’m sure will continue to happen. Through the creation process, I’ve found certain pathways are unrealistic and clumsy to achieve. I’ve addressed some issues and inconsistencies in how a reader traverses the work. I’ve also added more branches from the key plot points. I like the idea of returning to these key points and being offered a false way out before losing control again. I am much happier with this new map and believe it offers a more complete vision of the events and rooms of the work. 

Through remapping and refining my understanding of the work, I have a much better idea of how it functions and how it escalates over time. I’m also far more aware of the limitations of my project thanks to becoming more familiar with TWINE Harlowe. I’ve decided to use TWINE as I’ve familiarised myself with it through experimentation both in class and at home. I have over four rooms complete but continue to tweak their design and formatting elements. I am yet to be fully satisfied with them but am enjoying configuring soundtracks, sound FX, background images. I’ve begun to explore animations and moving textboxes, that can assist in conveying a state of psychosis. I am also figuring out how to realise my goals of randomisation by trawling through the guidebooks and forums. I have established a few methods for achieving this, that I will have to experiment with further.
